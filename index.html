What I do (Research) >

Multiple infection, coinfection, and Virophage

When multiple viruses infect the same microbial cell, it allows viral interactions that can alter ecology and evolution at multiple trophic scales. I am interested in the dynamics of multiple infection. My interest spans across length scales from within the cell looking at decision-making and at the population-level looking at spatial clustering of populations driving multiple infections. A major goal of studying multiple infection is to understand cheating and cooperation at a fundamental biological scale. An emerging model system for virus coinfection is the virophage, a virus of a virus. This work has included collaboration with experimentalist Matthias Fischer of the Max Planck Institute
